Featured project · Master’s thesis

Pedestrian Detection, Tracking and Intention Prediction

Risk-aware navigation support for an autonomous grocery delivery robot operating in environments shared with pedestrians.

Problem and objective

Safer Navigation Around Pedestrians

Autonomous delivery robots operate close to people and must react safely to changing pedestrian behavior.

The objective was to develop a perception and prediction pipeline that could detect pedestrians, understand their current activity and estimate whether their movement state was likely to change within the next few seconds.

Input data

Robot and Environment Data

  • Depth-camera data
  • GPS data
  • Robot odometry
  • Pedestrian position and motion information
  • Temporal sequences of pedestrian behavior

Pipeline stage 1

Pedestrian Detection and Tracking

The first stage detected pedestrians near the robot and tracked them while they remained inside the relevant operating area.

This created the spatial and temporal information required by the later action-recognition and intention-prediction stages.

Pipeline stage 2

Action Recognition with I3D

An Inflated 3D neural-network model was used to classify the pedestrian’s current action state.

  • Standing still
  • Walking or moving

This stage classified current behavior; it was separate from the prediction of future intention.

Pipeline stage 3

Intention Prediction with LSTM and MLP

An LSTM model processed temporal behavior and motion information to estimate whether a pedestrian was likely to change their current action state.

A final multilayer perceptron layer produced a probability estimate for the predicted state change.

Intended application

Risk-Aware Path-Planning Support

The predicted behavior could help the robot identify pedestrians who may soon become relevant obstacles and support more anticipatory navigation decisions.

The project connected perception, deep learning, temporal prediction and human-aware mobile robotics.

Depth Camera · GPS · Odometry · I3D · LSTM · MLP · Deep Learning · Human-Robot Interaction
