Featured research project · August 2024–February 2025

Intention Recognition of Vulnerable Road Users in the Frame of Human-Centered Decision Making for Autonomous Driving

Master’s thesis on pedestrian perception and short-term intention prediction for safer decision support in an autonomous delivery-robot scenario.

Problem and objective

Human-Centered Autonomous Decision Making

Autonomous delivery robots operate close to vulnerable road users and must react safely to changes in pedestrian behavior.

I developed a perception and prediction pipeline that detected and tracked pedestrians, classified their current action and estimated whether their movement state was likely to change.

Perception pipeline

Detection, Tracking and Sensor Data

  • Pedestrian detection and tracking
  • Depth-camera data
  • GPS and robot odometry
  • Spatial and temporal pedestrian information
  • Data preparation for action and intention models

Deep-learning models

I3D, LSTM and MLP

  • I3D for current-action recognition
  • Classification of standing and moving states
  • LSTM for temporal prediction of state changes
  • MLP layer for intention-probability estimation
  • Separation of current action from future intention

Application and milestones

Risk-Aware Navigation Support

The predicted behavior was intended to support more anticipatory and risk-aware path-planning decisions around pedestrians.

  • First presentation at THD Campus Cham: April 2025
  • Final presentation and delivery of code and results: June 2025

Visual project evidence

Perception and intention-prediction results

The images show the delivery-robot application context, the depth-based pedestrian-perception stage and a temporal example in which the probability of a walking pedestrian changing state increases before the current-action classifier fully changes its decision.

Depth Camera · GPS · Odometry · I3D · LSTM · MLP · Deep Learning · Human-Robot Interaction
